From precision farming and AI-powered advisory to supply chain digitisation and agri-fintech, <strong>the top agritech startups</strong> in India are rewriting the rules of farming.</p>



<p>This guide covers 17 standout agritech startups shaping Indian agriculture in 2026 — including their founding stories, features, pros, cons, and market positions.</p>

<h2>DeHaat</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="DeHaat website" class="wp-image-9567" height="1114" src="https://decentro.tech/blog/wp-content/uploads/Screenshot-2026-03-24-at-3.45.36-PM.png" width="2860" /></figure>



<p><strong>HQ:</strong> Gurugram, Haryana | <strong>Founded:</strong> 2012 | <strong>Total Funding:</strong> $270M+</p>



<p><a href="https://agrevolution.in/" rel="noreferrer noopener nofollow" target="_blank">DeHaat (Green Agrevolution Pvt. Ltd.</a>) is India&#8217;s most funded full-stack agritech platform, serving small and marginal farmers across 12 Indian states. Founded by Shashank Kumar, Shyam Sundar Singh, Amrendra Singh, Adarsh Srivastava, and Abhishek Dokania, DeHaat offers a complete suite of services — from agri-input procurement and expert advisory to credit facilitation and market linkages. In FY25, it crossed ₹3,000 crore in revenue (an 11% YoY increase) and reported a net profit of ₹369 crore. It exports to 32 markets, including the UK, Europe, and Southeast Asia, targeting ₹800 crore in export revenues in FY26. The company raised ₹200 crore in venture debt from Trifecta Capital in April 2025 and acquired agri-advisory app AgriCentral.</p>



<p><strong>Top Features:</strong></p>



<ul><li>End-to-end agri services: inputs, advisory, credit, and market linkages<br /></li><li>AgriCentral app for crop disease detection and expert advisory<br /></li><li>Drone-based farming services via partnership with Drone Destination<br /></li><li>Agrifood consumer brand for supply chain integration<br /></li><li>Export linkages to 32+ global markets<br /></li></ul>



<p><strong>Pros:</strong></p>



<ul><li>India&#8217;s largest agritech by revenue; crossed ₹3,000 crore in FY25<br /></li><li>First profitable year in FY25 (operational profitability path being pursued)<br /></li><li>Strongest funding pedigree in Indian agritech ($270M+, backed by Temasek, Sofina, Peak XV)<br /></li></ul>



<p><strong>Cons:</strong></p>



<ul><li>Operational profitability is still elusive without accounting for fair-value CCPS adjustments.<br /></li><li>Growth relies heavily on agri-input sales, which have thin margins.<br /></li><li>Presence concentrated in 12 states — pan-India scale yet to be achieved.</li></ul>



<h2>Ninjacart</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="Ninjacart website" class="wp-image-9568" height="1296" src="https://decentro.tech/blog/wp-content/uploads/Screenshot-2026-03-24-at-3.47.33-PM.png" width="2862" /></figure>



<p><strong>HQ:</strong> Bengaluru, Karnataka | <strong>Founded:</strong> 2015 | <strong>Total Funding:</strong> $407M+</p>



<p><a href="https://ninjacart.com/" rel="noreferrer noopener nofollow" target="_blank">Ninjacart</a> is India&#8217;s largest B2B fresh produce supply chain company, digitising the link between farmers, retailers, and restaurants using tech-enabled logistics and demand forecasting. Backed by Tiger Global, Walmart, and Flipkart, it serves thousands of farmers and connects them with urban retail buyers, reducing waste. In 2025, Ninjacart expanded into agri-fintech and lending services for farmers and buyers alike.</p>



<p><strong>Top Features:</strong></p>



<ul><li>Real-time demand-supply matching for fresh produce<br /></li><li>AI-powered inventory and logistics optimization<br /></li><li>Direct farm-to-retailer routing to eliminate intermediaries<br /></li><li>Embedded fintech for buyer credit and farmer advances<br /></li><li>Quality grading at the farm gate to reduce rejection rates<br /></li></ul>



<p><strong>Pros:</strong></p>



<ul><li>Largest-ever agritech funding at $407M+<br /></li><li>Backed by marquee investors, including Walmart and Flipkart<br /></li><li>Significant reduction in food wastage through tech-optimised cold chain logistics<br /></li></ul>



<p><strong>Cons:</strong></p>



<ul><li>The fresh produce supply chain is inherently capital-intensive and operationally complex.<br /></li><li>Profitability timeline remains extended due to high logistics costs.<br /></li><li>Concentration in metro and tier-1 cities limits rural farmer reach</li></ul>



<h2>AgroStar</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="AgroStar website" class="wp-image-9569" height="1302" src="https://decentro.tech/blog/wp-content/uploads/Screenshot-2026-03-24-at-3.48.27-PM.png" width="2868" /></figure>



<p><strong>HQ:</strong> Pune, Maharashtra | <strong>Founded:</strong> 2013 | <strong>Total Funding:</strong> $111M+</p>



<p><a href="https://corporate.agrostar.in/" rel="noreferrer noopener nofollow" target="_blank">AgroStar</a> is India&#8217;s largest digital farmer network and agri-inputs platform, serving over 5 million farmers across Gujarat, Rajasthan, Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, and Uttar Pradesh. It combines a digital advisory platform with an agri-input marketplace. In November 2025, AgroStar raised $30 million led by global climate investment firm Just Climate, signalling a strong pivot toward sustainable and climate-resilient agriculture.</p>



<p><strong>Top Features:</strong></p>



<ul><li>Digital advisory platform for crop disease and pest management<br /></li><li>Agri-input marketplace (seeds, fertilisers, pesticides, machinery)<br /></li><li>AgroStar app with vernacular language support for farmers<br /></li><li>Agronomist-on-call service for expert recommendations<br /></li><li>Climate-smart agronomy modules (post-Just Climate investment)<br /></li></ul>



<p><strong>Pros:</strong></p>



<ul><li>India&#8217;s largest farmer network with 5M+ active users.<br /></li><li>$30M fresh funding from Just Climate reinforces long-term sustainability vision.<br /></li><li>Strong distribution depth across Tier-2 and Tier-3 geographies<br /></li></ul>



<p><strong>Cons:</strong></p>



<ul><li>Revenue model dependent on agri-input e-commerce, which faces price competition from offline channels<br /></li><li>Reaching the &#8220;last mile&#8221; of rural India remains logistically expensive<br /></li><li>Competition from DeHaat and BharatAgri is intensifying in the advisory space</li></ul>



<h2>Arya.ag</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="Arya.ag website" class="wp-image-9570" height="1014" src="https://decentro.tech/blog/wp-content/uploads/Screenshot-2026-03-24-at-3.51.25-PM.png" width="2880" /></figure>



<p><strong>HQ:</strong> New Delhi | <strong>Founded:</strong> 2013 | <strong>Total Funding:</strong> ~$115M (incl. ₹725Cr Series D)</p>



<p><a href="http://arya.ag" rel="noreferrer noopener nofollow" target="_blank">Arya.ag</a> is India&#8217;s largest integrated grain commerce platform, operating over 5.0 million tonnes of storage capacity across 5,500 warehouses in 21 Indian states. In January 2026, it raised ₹725 crore (~$80.6M) in a Series D round led by GEF Capital Partners, one of the largest agritech funding rounds of 2026. The platform reported ₹31.5 crore in profit for H1 FY26 and is deploying the capital to build 12,000 agri-warehouses and 100 Smart Farm Centres.</p>



<p><strong>Top Features:</strong></p>



<ul><li>Post-harvest storage: 5.5M+ tonne capacity across 5,500+ warehouses<br /></li><li>Warehouse Receipt Financing (WRF) for farmers to access credit against stored grain<br /></li><li>Smart Farm Centres for last-mile agri-services<br /></li><li>Climate-smart grain commerce tools to help farmers time their sales<br /></li><li>Integrated FPO (Farmer-Producer Organisation) engagement<br /></li></ul>



<p><strong>Pros:</strong></p>



<ul><li>Profitable business in H1 FY26, rare among Indian agritech startups<br /></li><li>Largest grain warehousing network in India with 5M+ tonne capacity<br /></li><li>₹725 crore Series D in 2026 ranks among the largest recent agritech rounds<br /></li></ul>



<p><strong>Cons:</strong></p>



<ul><li>Warehouse infrastructure is capital-intensive with long payback periods.<br /></li><li>Strong dependency on commodity price cycles, which are volatile<br /></li><li>Currently focused on grain, diversification into other crops is still in the early stages</li></ul>



<h2>CropIn</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="CropIn website" class="wp-image-9571" height="1236" src="https://decentro.tech/blog/wp-content/uploads/Screenshot-2026-03-24-at-3.52.29-PM.png" width="2880" /></figure>

<p><a href="https://www.cropin.com/" rel="noreferrer noopener nofollow" target="_blank">CropIn</a> is a pioneering farm data intelligence company that transforms farm-level data into actionable insights for agribusinesses, banks, insurers, and governments. Founded by Krishna Kumar, CropIn&#8217;s AI-powered platform has digitised millions of acres of farmland globally and is used by over 250 agribusiness clients across 52 countries. Its flagship product, SmartFarm, provides end-to-end crop lifecycle monitoring using satellite imagery, IoT sensors, and machine learning.</p>



<p><strong>Top Features:</strong></p>



<ul><li>SmartFarm: crop lifecycle management and farm digitisation<br /></li><li>SmartRisk: AI-driven crop risk assessment for banks and insurers<br /></li><li>SmartAcre: satellite-based remote sensing for large-scale crop monitoring<br /></li><li>Agri Data Platform with real-time weather, soil, and crop intelligence<br /></li><li>Global coverage across 52 countries<br /></li></ul>



<p><strong>Pros:</strong></p>



<ul><li>First Indian agritech company to deploy AI at a global scale across 52 countries<br /></li><li>Trusted by 250+ agribusiness clients, including banks and governments<br /></li><li>Critical enabler for agricultural lending and crop insurance underwriting<br /></li></ul>



<p><strong>Cons:</strong></p>



<ul><li>The B2B SaaS model means direct farmer impact is indirect<br /></li><li>Scaling in fragmented markets with limited data infrastructure is challenging.<br /></li><li>Faces competition from global precision agriculture platforms</li></ul>



<h2>Fasal</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="Fasal website" class="wp-image-9572" height="1350" src="https://decentro.tech/blog/wp-content/uploads/Screenshot-2026-03-24-at-3.56.17-PM.png" width="2868" /></figure>



<p><strong>HQ:</strong> Bengaluru, Karnataka | <strong>Founded:</strong> 2018 | <strong>Total Funding:</strong> $15M+</p>



<p><a href="https://www.fasal.co/" rel="noreferrer noopener nofollow" target="_blank">Fasal (Wolkus Technology Solutions)</a> is an IoT-led precision farming platform designed specifically for horticulture crops such as grapes, tomatoes, pomegranates, and capsicum. Its proprietary IoT devices are deployed directly in the farm to capture real-time microclimate data, which is then analysed by its AI engine to provide crop-specific, time-sensitive recommendations.</p>



<p><strong>Top Features:</strong></p>



<ul><li>Farm-level IoT sensors for soil moisture, temperature, humidity, and leaf wetness<br /></li><li>AI-driven crop advisory based on real-time microclimate data<br /></li><li>Disease prediction models for high-value horticulture crops<br /></li><li>Integration with irrigation systems for automated water management<br /></li><li>Mobile app with actionable notifications for Indian smallholder farmers<br /></li></ul>



<p><strong>Pros:</strong></p>



<ul><li>Only Indian platform offering farm-level IoT advisory specifically for horticulture<br /></li><li>Demonstrated yield improvements of 20–35% in pilot deployments<br /></li><li>Strong appeal to progressive farmers in Maharashtra, Karnataka, and Andhra Pradesh<br /></li></ul>



<p><strong>Cons:</strong></p>



<ul><li>IoT hardware deployment has upfront cost barriers for small farmers<br /></li><li>Focused on horticulture — limited addressable market compared to staple crops<br /></li><li>Hardware maintenance and connectivity in rural areas remain challenges</li></ul>



<h2>Bijak</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="Bijak website" class="wp-image-9573" height="1336" src="https://decentro.tech/blog/wp-content/uploads/Screenshot-2026-03-24-at-3.56.51-PM.png" width="2878" /></figure>

<h2>AgNext Technologies</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="AgNext Technologies website" class="wp-image-9574" height="1336" src="https://decentro.tech/blog/wp-content/uploads/Screenshot-2026-03-24-at-3.57.36-PM.png" width="2878" /></figure>

<p><strong>Top Features:</strong></p>



<ul><li>Qualix: instant AI-based quality testing for 30+ agri-commodities<br /></li><li>Deployed at grain mandis, procurement centres, and food processing units<br /></li><li>Portable and stationary device versions for field and lab use<br /></li><li>Integration with commodity trading platforms and government procurement systems<br /></li><li>Reduces quality disputes in agri-commodity trade by providing objective data<br /></li></ul>

<ul><li>Solves a critical pain point: subjective quality assessment costs Indian farmers billions every year<br /></li><li>Deployed in government commodity procurement (FCI, state agencies)<br /></li><li>Strong IP moat in spectroscopy-based commodity quality testing<br /></li></ul>



<p><strong>Cons:</strong></p>



<ul><li>A hardware-dependent model requires significant deployment and maintenance investment<br /></li><li>Scaling across India&#8217;s 7,000+ mandis is a monumental challenge<br /></li><li>Revenue visibility and scale metrics are not publicly disclosed</li></ul>

This merger creates one of India&#8217;s more significant full-stack agritech plays, combining input distribution, digital advisory, and rural trade in Madhya Pradesh and Rajasthan. Gramophone had previously raised ₹55 crore in total funding from Info Edge, Asha Impact, and Better Capital. Post-merger, Unnati&#8217;s expanded platform serves farmers across agri-advisory, inputs, credit, and crop commerce through a single integrated platform.</p>

<p><strong>Pros:</strong></p>

<p><strong>Cons:</strong></p>



<ul><li>Post-acquisition integration carries execution risks in product, people, and operations<br /></li><li>Brand consolidation between Gramophone and Unnati is still underway<br /></li><li>No large external funding round post-merger announced yet</li></ul>



<h2>FarmERP</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="FarmERP website" class="wp-image-9577" height="1338" src="https://decentro.tech/blog/wp-content/uploads/Screenshot-2026-03-24-at-4.05.05-PM.png" width="2880" /></figure>
