<p>India&#8217;s auto loan market is on a serious tear. Valued at <strong><a href="https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/india-auto-loan-market" rel="noreferrer noopener nofollow" target="_blank">USD 46.33 billion in 2026</a></strong> and projected to hit USD 63.82 billion by 2031, the vehicle financing space has evolved far beyond the branch-visit-and-paperwork era. Today, it is powered by AI-driven underwriting, Aadhaar-based e-KYC, co-lending partnerships, and mobile-first platforms that can disburse loans in under 24 hours.</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="Used car loans segment insight" class="wp-image-9709" height="1365" src="https://decentro.tech/blog/wp-content/uploads/Listicle-CarFinance_Insight.jpg" width="2500" /></figure>



<p>Whether you&#8217;re a consumer looking to buy your first hatchback, a fleet operator financing 50 electric three-wheelers, or a fintech company building lending infrastructure, choosing the right <strong>vehicle financing platform</strong> can make all the difference. NBFCs, banks, and fintech startups are each carving out distinct niches — and the competition has never been more fierce.</p>



<p>This guide rounds up the <strong>15 best vehicle financing platforms in 2026</strong>, with a strong focus on Indian players who are reshaping how the country buys, sells, and finances vehicles.</p>

<h2>Rupyy (CarDekho Group)</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="Rupyy (CarDekho Group) website" class="wp-image-9689" height="1316" src="https://decentro.tech/blog/wp-content/uploads/Screenshot-2026-04-30-at-1.41.11 PM.png" width="2880" /></figure>



<p><strong>Founded:</strong> 2022 (financial services arm active since 2016) <strong>Headquarters:</strong> Gurugram, India</p>



<p>Rupyy is the digital lending arm of <a href="https://www.cardekho.com/" rel="noreferrer noopener nofollow" target="_blank">CarDekho Group</a> — one of India&#8217;s leading automotive unicorns. Built specifically to democratize vehicle financing, Rupyy covers new cars, used cars, two-wheelers, EVs, and loans against cars. It partners with more than 37 lenders including HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, Axis Bank, Kotak Mahindra, and YES Bank — and its dealer network reaches 90% of India&#8217;s pin codes.</p>



<p>As of mid-2023, Rupyy clocked an annualised loan disbursement run rate of INR 12,000 crore with 88% year-on-year revenue growth. It commands a <strong>15% market share in the pre-owned vehicle loan segment</strong>, making it one of the most dominant digital vehicle financing platforms in the country.</p>



<h4>Top Features</h4>



<ul><li>End-to-end digital loan journey from application to disbursement</li><li>37+ lending partners covering banks, NBFCs, and Rupyy&#8217;s own NBFC book</li><li>Pan-India distribution across 1,500+ locations and 9,000+ distributor touchpoints</li><li>EV financing partnerships with Hero Electric, Ampere, Tork, Pure EV, and others</li><li>Loan-against-car and personal loan products for broader financial inclusion</li><li>Proprietary credit underwriting for thin-file and non-metro borrowers</li></ul>



<h4>Pros</h4>



<ul><li>Deepest distribution network among digital auto-lending platforms</li><li>Covers new, used, two-wheeler, and EV financing under one roof</li><li>Real-time loan quotes from multiple lenders enable competitive comparisons</li><li>Strong Tier II and Tier III city penetration</li></ul>



<h4>Cons</h4>



<ul><li>Primarily a marketplace; borrowers may receive varying rates based on lender</li><li>EV financing portfolio still maturing compared to used car stronghold</li></ul>



<h4>Market Share</h4>



<p>~15% in pre-owned vehicle loans; targeting 10% share in EV two-wheeler financing over the next 5 years.</p>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="CARS24 Financial Services website" class="wp-image-9690" height="1316" src="https://decentro.tech/blog/wp-content/uploads/Screenshot-2026-04-30-at-1.41.57 PM.png" width="2880" /></figure>

<h4>Cons</h4>



<ul><li>Financing primarily restricted to pre-owned cars transacted within the CARS24 ecosystem</li><li>Interest rates may be higher for borrowers with lower credit profiles</li></ul>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="Shriram Finance website" class="wp-image-9691" height="1362" src="https://decentro.tech/blog/wp-content/uploads/Screenshot-2026-04-30-at-1.42.57 PM.png" width="2876" /></figure>

<p><a href="https://www.shriramfinance.in/" rel="noreferrer noopener nofollow" target="_blank">Shriram Finance</a> is India&#8217;s largest retail NBFC by assets under management in the vehicle financing segment. After the landmark merger of Shriram Transport Finance, Shriram City Union Finance, and Shriram Capital in 2023, the entity crossed <strong>INR 2.5 trillion (₹2.5 lakh crore) in AUM</strong>, cementing its dominance in commercial vehicle (CV) and used vehicle finance. In January 2025, Montra Electric partnered with Shriram Finance for customised EV loan products.</p>



<h4>Top Features</h4>



<ul><li>Specialises in used commercial vehicle (CV) financing — trucks, buses, and cargo vans</li><li>AUM exceeding ₹2.6 trillion across vehicle, MSME, gold, and tractor loans</li><li>Deep penetration in Tier II, III, and rural markets</li><li>AI-driven risk scoring and digital paperless loan journeys</li><li>EV credit line: USD 150 million ADB facility targeting MSME and EV expansion (March 2025)</li></ul>



<h4>Pros</h4>



<ul><li>Unmatched rural and semi-urban presence for commercial vehicle loans</li><li>Post-merger scale enables lower cost of funds</li><li>Consistently strong collection efficiency and portfolio quality</li></ul>



<h4>Cons</h4>



<ul><li>Primarily suited for commercial vehicle operators; less competitive for personal passenger car loans</li><li>Gross NPA in mid-tier NBFC vehicle loans can be a watch area</li></ul>



<h4>Market Share</h4>



<p>Largest NBFC vehicle lender by AUM in India; dominates the used commercial vehicle segment with an estimated 18–20% share in its focused categories.</p>

<h3>Emerging Trends Reshaping Vehicle Financing in 2026</h3>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="Auto Loan outstanding growth YOY growth" class="wp-image-9707" height="1457" src="https://decentro.tech/blog/wp-content/uploads/Listicle-CarFinance_Outstanding_Growth.jpg" width="2500" /></figure>



<p>The vehicle financing landscape in India is undergoing its most significant transformation in decades. A few key trends are worth calling out:</p>



<p><strong>EV Financing Goes Mainstream:</strong>&nbsp;</p>



<p>With India&#8217;s EV financing market expected to grow at a 51.62% CAGR to reach USD 28.79 billion by 2031, almost every lender from <a href="https://www.bajajfinserv.in/">Bajaj Finance</a> to <a href="https://revfin.in/">RevFin</a> is building EV-specific products. OEM-subsidized rates and government-backed incentives are compressing costs.</p>



<p><strong>Account Aggregator &amp; Digital KYC:</strong>&nbsp;</p>



<p>RBI&#8217;s Account Aggregator framework and Digital Lending Directions 2025 mandate standardized e-KYC and transparent pricing, making it easier for lenders to underwrite borrowers with non-traditional income without sacrificing compliance.</p>



<p><strong>Co-Lending Proliferation:</strong></p>



<p>&nbsp;Banks and NBFCs are increasingly co-lending — with <a href="https://www.hdfcbank.com/">HDFC Bank</a> and Axis Bank providing low-cost funds alongside NBFCs that contribute field origination and last-mile servicing.</p>



<p><strong>Online Direct Lending Boom:</strong>&nbsp;</p>



<p>While dealership point-of-sale dominated 67.58% of India&#8217;s auto loan market in 2025, online direct lending is growing at an 8.78% CAGR through 2031, driven by instant approvals and rate transparency.</p>

<h1 class="alignfull has-text-align-center">Frequently Asked Questions</h1>



<p><strong>What is a vehicle financing platform?</strong> </p>



<p>A vehicle financing platform is a digital or hybrid lender, marketplace, or NBFC that provides loans, leases, or credit facilities to help individuals or businesses purchase vehicles — including cars, bikes, trucks, and EVs.</p>



<p><strong>Which is the best vehicle financing platform in India in 2026?</strong> </p>



<p><a href="https://www.rupyy.com/">Rupyy</a>, <a href="https://www.cars24.com/">CARS24 Financial Services</a>, <a href="https://www.shriramfinance.in/">Shriram Finance</a>, and <a href="https://www.mahindrafinance.com/">Mahindra Finance</a> are among the best vehicle financing platforms in India, each excelling in different segments — digital used car loans, commercial vehicles, and rural markets respectively.</p>



<p><strong>What is the size of the vehicle financing market in India?</strong> </p>



<p>The India auto loan market is valued at approximately USD 46.33 billion in 2026 and is projected to grow to USD 63.82 billion by 2031 at a CAGR of 6.62%.</p>



<p><strong>How are NBFCs different from banks in vehicle financing?</strong> </p>



<p>NBFCs typically offer more flexible credit assessment criteria, faster processing, and better service in Tier II/III markets. Banks offer lower interest rates but have stricter eligibility requirements.</p>



<p><strong>Are there dedicated EV financing platforms in India?</strong> </p>



<p>Yes. <a href="https://revfin.in/">RevFin</a>, Ecofy, and lending arms of <a href="https://www.bajajfinserv.in/">Bajaj Finance</a> and <a href="https://www.mahindrafinance.com/">Mahindra Finance</a> are actively building EV-specific financing products, supported by government subsidies and green financing facilities.</p>
